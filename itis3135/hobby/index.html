What is Boxing?

Boxing is a sport that involves two individuals, typically wearing the appropriate protective gear, who throw punches at each other in a ring. The objective is to score points by landing punches on the opponent while avoiding being hit.

Boxing is one of the oldest combat sports, with evidence dating back to at least 3000 BCE. Its formal development began in ancient Greece, where it was an official Olympic sport in 688 BCE.

Why I do it?

I box for several reasons. First and foremost, I love the sport itself. The combination of physical and mental challenges keeps me engaged and motivated to improve.

The second reason is that it brings people together. Boxing has a unique way of fostering camaraderie and teamwork among those who do the sport. It creates lifelong friendships and bonds that extend beyond the ring. You beat each other up and hug it out afterwards.

Finally, the most IMPORTANT reason... my mom. She joined me when I first started boxing and next thing you know, she's in my corner at the Junior Olympics.
